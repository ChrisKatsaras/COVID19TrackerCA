COVID-19 Vaccinations in Canada

more than doses of the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine have been administered across Canada.


As provinces across Canada begin their COVID-19 vaccination programs, we're tracking every dose administered. Today, at least doses were administered.

The COVID-19 vaccine currently approved for use in Canada requires two doses, 21 days apart, for full efficacy. Consequently, the values we report indicate the total number of doses administered. We also report 'equivalent people vaccinated', a representation of the number of people who could be fully vaccinated with the doses already administered. As of today, people could be completely vaccinated with the doses already administered.

It's important to note that we can only share an incomplete picture of COVID-19 vaccinations in Canada at this time. For most provinces, there's no regular reporting of vaccination data, in contrast to other COVID-19 related data. As such, much of our data comes from press releases and live briefings, and many provinces may not provide daily updates - especially during weekends and holidays.
